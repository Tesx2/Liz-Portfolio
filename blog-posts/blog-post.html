Finding Flow in the Creative Process

By Liz Kendi • October 12, 2025

Creativity isn’t always a lightning strike of inspiration — it’s often a rhythm, a flow that you nurture over time. As a designer and animator, I’ve learned that the most fulfilling work happens when I’m fully immersed, when ideas move naturally and effortlessly from thought to design.

One of the keys to finding this flow is creating an environment that encourages it. For me, that means setting aside dedicated creative time, curating playlists that match the project’s mood, and allowing myself to experiment freely before seeking perfection.

I’ve also found that the best ideas come from outside the screen — taking walks, observing colors in nature, or sketching loosely on paper. These moments of disconnect often lead to deeper connections in the work.

“Flow happens when creativity meets focus — when you lose track of time and gain clarity of vision.”

Whether I’m designing a brand identity or animating a sequence, I aim to stay open, curious, and connected to the story I’m telling. That’s where true creative magic lives.
